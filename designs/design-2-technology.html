The Platform

One system, four outputs, continuous power

AI coordinates solar generation, electrolysis, fuel cells, and thermal management as a single regenerative cycle — enabling power, hydrogen, oxygen, and water production anywhere on the lunar surface.

Regenerative Energy Cycle

Storage and production, in one loop

During lunar day, solar arrays generate more power than a payload needs. Rather than dumping that excess, our platform routes it into an electrolyzer, splitting stored water into hydrogen and oxygen.

Through the 14-day lunar night, that hydrogen and oxygen run back through a fuel cell to generate continuous power — producing water as a byproduct that feeds back into the cycle. Nothing is a dead end; every output becomes an input somewhere else in the system.

AI-Driven Management

Software decides how the cycle runs

The physical hardware is only half the system. An onboard AI continuously models power demand, thermal load, and consumable reserves, then decides in real time how much to store, produce, or discharge.

Predictive load balancing

Anticipates a payload's power draw ahead of the lunar night, adjusting electrolysis rates so reserves are never over- or under-built.

Autonomous fault response

Detects component degradation or thermal anomalies and reroutes the cycle automatically, without waiting on ground control.

Mission-specific tuning

The same control software adapts to a rover's short bursts of demand or a habitat's steady baseline load, without new hardware.

Core Technologies

The hardware underneath the cycle

Fuel cells and electrolyzers are the enabling technologies — not the product. The product is the system that coordinates them.

01

Fuel Cell

Converts stored hydrogen and oxygen back into electrical power through the lunar night, with water as a recoverable byproduct.

02

Electrolyzer

Splits water into hydrogen and oxygen using surplus solar power during the lunar day, charging the system for the night ahead.

03

Thermal Management

Regulates extreme swings between lunar day and night so the fuel cell, electrolyzer, and storage tanks stay within operating range.

Why This, Not Existing Solutions

Advantages over the status quo

vs. Battery-only systems

Batteries alone can't carry a payload through a 14-day lunar night at reasonable mass. Our cycle regenerates power instead of only storing it.

vs. Single-output power systems

Most power systems deliver electricity alone. Ours produces hydrogen, oxygen, and water as part of the same cycle, at no added mass.

vs. Mission-specific power design

Instead of a custom power solution per mission, our modular architecture scales across rovers, habitats, and ISRU sites without redesign.
